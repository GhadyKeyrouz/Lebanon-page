A Taste of Lebanon

The Most Famous Lebanese Dishes

Kibbeh

Often considered Lebanon’s national dish, kibbeh is made from bulgur, minced meat, and spices. It can be fried, baked, or even served raw in some traditional styles.

Tabbouleh

A fresh and vibrant salad made with parsley, bulgur, tomatoes, mint, lemon juice, and olive oil. It’s one of the most famous Lebanese dishes and a staple on almost every table.

Hummus

A creamy blend of chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic, hummus is one of Lebanon’s most loved dishes and is enjoyed as a dip or side with bread.

Fattoush

A refreshing Lebanese salad made with crispy toasted pita bread, lettuce, tomatoes, cucumber, radish, and sumac. It’s light, colorful, and full of flavor.

Shawarma

Thin slices of marinated meat, usually chicken or beef, slow-cooked on a vertical spit and served in a wrap or sandwich with garlic sauce, pickles, and vegetables.

Manakish

Often called the Lebanese version of pizza, manakish is flatbread topped with ingredients like zaatar, cheese, or minced meat and is especially popular for breakfast.

Warak Enab

These are grape leaves stuffed with rice, herbs, and sometimes meat. They’re a classic part of Lebanese mezze and are loved for their tangy and savory taste.

Falafel

Crispy fried patties made from chickpeas or fava beans, usually served in pita bread with vegetables, pickles, and tahini sauce.

Kafta

A flavorful dish made from seasoned minced meat mixed with parsley, onions, and spices. It can be grilled or baked and served with rice, bread, or potatoes or even served raw.

Baklava

A rich and sweet dessert made from layers of flaky pastry, nuts, and syrup. It’s one of the most well-known Lebanese sweets.

Aish El Saraya

Aish El Saraya is a traditional Lebanese dessert made with sweet bread, cream, and syrup, often topped with pistachios. It has a soft, rich texture and a delicate flavor from rose or orange blossom water.
